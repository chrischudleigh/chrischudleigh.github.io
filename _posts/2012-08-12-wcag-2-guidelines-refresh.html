There are 4 principles with 12 guidelines arranged under these principles:

  1. perceivable
    1. Provide text alternatives for non-text content (text alternatives) (1)
    2. Provide captions and other alternatives for multimedia (time-based media) (5)
    3. Create content that can be presented in different ways (adaptable - semantics) (3)
    4. Make it easier for users to see and hear content (distinguishable) (5)
  2. operable
    1. Make all functionality available from the keyboard (keyboard accessible) (2)
    2. Give users enough time to read and use content (enough time) (2)
    3. Do not use content that causes seizures(seizures) (1)
    4. Help users navigate and find content (navigable) (7)
  3. understandable
    1. Make text readable and understandable (readable) (2)
    2. Make content appear and operate in predictable ways (predictable) (4)
    3. Help users avoid and correct mistakes (input assistance) (4)
  4. robust
    1. Maximise compatibility with current and future user tools (parsing - validation) (2)

There are then 38 testable success criteria to meet AA requirements.
